Earthquake magnitudes are shown as circles,
with radius as 2^magnitude. Magnitudes are
actually exponents of ten; this compromise
shows more variation than a linear scale but
lets small circles be visible while large ones
still fit on the page. So far.

Tectonic plate boundaries (magenta) from
https://github.com/fraxen/tectonicplates, with
data from Hugo Ahlenius, Nordpil, and Peter Bird.

Click on earthquakes for details of time,
location, and magnitude.
